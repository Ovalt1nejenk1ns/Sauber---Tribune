Top Story

Spring Arrives: The Season of Greatest Promise for Garden & Workshop Alike

Staff Correspondent

With the arrival of March, the industrious homesteader finds himself pulled in most agreeable directions simultaneously. The garden beds call for preparation, the Wheel Horse emerges from its winter slumber requiring careful inspection, the workshop fills again with the fragrant smell of fresh lumber, and the auction season stirs to life with the promise of remarkable finds.

The wise steward of land and shop understands that each season imposes its own logic upon the calendar. Now is the time to start tomatoes and peppers indoors — some six to eight weeks before the last frost expected around the fifteenth of April.

"The garden that is not planned in winter is already half-lost by summer."

For those possessed of a Wheel Horse tractor, spring service is not merely advisable but essential. A morning spent in diligent attention now is worth a week of frustration come cutting season.

What to Do This Season

🌸 Spring Tasks

  • Start tomatoes & peppers indoors
  • Prepare & amend garden beds
  • Plant peas, lettuce, spinach outside
  • Full Wheel Horse pre-season service
  • Scout spring equipment auctions
  • Sharpen and set all edge tools

☀️ Summer Tasks

  • Consistent watering; apply mulch
  • Harvest cool-weather crops
  • Attend farm & estate auctions

🍂 Autumn Tasks

  • Plant garlic & perennial bulbs
  • Estate auction season peaks
  • Winterize tractor for storage

❄️ Winter Tasks

  • Plan next year's garden layout
  • Order seeds early
  • Workshop time — big projects

Local Guidance

The Columbus Gardener's Calendar: Zone 6a in Full

Garden Correspondent

The Hilliard and Columbus environs occupy USDA Hardiness Zone 6a. The last spring frost arrives near the fifteenth of April, and the season closes around the fifteenth of October — providing some two hundred frost-free days for one's agricultural ambitions.

Those wishing to grow tomatoes, peppers, and other tender crops should begin seed-starting indoors six to eight weeks before the last frost date. The Old Farmer's Almanac's planting calendar, consulted with one's zip code in hand, provides precise sowing windows for every common vegetable.

"The garden is not merely a place of vegetables — it is a school of patience and a measure of a man's character."

Charles Dowding's no-dig approach — which suppresses weeds through cardboard and compost rather than tillage — has converted many a skeptic. MIgardener, a channel of Midwestern sensibility, speaks with authority on the challenges of growing in our region.

History

South Bend's Finest: A Brief History of the Wheel Horse

Mechanical Correspondent

The Wheel Horse tractor entered production in South Bend, Indiana in 1946 under the hand of Elmer Pond, whose ambition was to furnish the American homeowner with a machine of genuine quality. Toro acquired the brand in 1986 and continued production until 2006.

"A Wheel Horse properly maintained will outlast every machine built since the day it left South Bend."

Maintenance

The Seasonal Maintenance Register

🌸 Spring Pre-Season Service

  • Change engine oil and oil filter
  • Install fresh fuel; add stabilizer if stored
  • Clean or rebuild carburetor
  • Inspect and replace drive belts
  • Sharpen or replace mower blades
  • Grease all zerks and pivot points
  • Check battery voltage; clean terminals
  • Inspect and inflate tires

❄️ Winter Storage

  • Drain fuel or add fuel stabilizer
  • Fog cylinder with oil; remove spark plug
  • Remove battery; store indoors
  • Clean machine; cover with breathable tarp

Correspondent's Report

Know Your Price Before You Raise Your Paddle

Saleyard Reporter

The auction house rewards the prepared buyer and punishes the impulsive one with equal reliability. Before attending any sale, the wise bidder establishes his maximum price through independent research, then adheres to it with the conviction of a man who has learned this lesson before.

eBay's completed listings function provides the most reliable gauge of what items actually sell for. One searches, filters to completed listings, and examines the green figures to establish a realistic range.

"Set your price at home. The auctioneer's chant was designed to make you forget it."

The buyer's premium deserves particular attention in the era of online auctions. Most platforms add fifteen to eighteen percent atop the hammer price — factor this into every calculation before bidding.
